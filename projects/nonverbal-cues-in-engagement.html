Nonverbal Cues in Children’s Engagement During Reading

A study exploring the effect of parents’ nonverbal cues on their child’s engagement during a joint educational reading task.

Overview

This project was part of the Affective Computing course I took at MIT Media Lab's Affective Computing group. My team and I explored the role of parents’ nonverbal cues on their child’s engagement during an educational dialogic reading interaction. We did this through two smaller explorations: the first is understanding how much synchrony or alignment there is between the parent’s and the child’s affective states, and the second is developing methods to predict a child’s engagement using the parent’s nonverbal cues.
